About Me

From a kid catching frogs at a pond to researcher advocating public interests in sciences

Introducing Myself

Growing up in Daejeon, South Korea, I always enjoyed learning new things. As a kid, I was catching insects and animals, studying their behaviors, admiring the similarities and differences in the features suit for their enviornments. With many research facilities and national science museum in Daejeon, I was able to countinue growing my interests in sciences. Reading books related to science and the scientists became my hobby, which further led me to deeply fall in love with chemistry, physics and many great minds behind it. At the end of my middle school, I really wanted to live and study in U.S. Reason was that most the world-class research being reported in english with many world-class research facilities and well-known researchers in the world are in U.S. My parents helped me pursue my dream - I can only think of how difficult that decision must have been for my parents at that time. Thanks to my parents, I was able to live in many places and educations in U.S. including Utah, Connecticut and Pennylvania, where I was able to finish my Masters degree under guidence of Dr. Millstone in University of Pittsburgh.

As a Korean citizen, I've recently finished my three-year military conscription. Fortunately, I was eligible to perform my military service as a technical research personnel with a Korean environmental company Siontech Co., Ltd. Though this position still required me to follow many of the military restrictions and obligations as others, it allowed me an opportunity to apply my knowledge of Chemistry and perform ongoing research. During this time, I have had the opportunity to develop and engineer new methods of purifying water and recycling PET waste. I also wrote and submitted those same methods to the Korean patent office and multiple international patent offices.

Nobody ever figures out what life is all about, and it doesn't matter. Explore the world. — Richard Feynmann

Now that I have fulfilled my service to my country, I am excited for my next chapter to begin and to explore new opportunities. From a young age, I was lucky to have encounter so many brilliant people, diverse cultures, and fascinating body of thoughts -- I would like to continue to explore and learn about the world.
