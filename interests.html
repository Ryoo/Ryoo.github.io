My Interests

Introducing Myself

I was born in Daejeon, South Korea. Being a decent size city known for science and technology, Daejeon was a great place to grow my passion and interest in science. Before graduating middle school, I wanted to pursue my passion for science by studying abroad in United States. To me, the ability to speak fluent English is important both in learning the world outside of my own and conducting research with the world-class researchers. With my parents full support, I was able to live in many places in U.S. including Utah, Connecticut and Pennylvania. During those times in U.S., I was able to finish my Masters degree under guidence of Dr. Millstone in University of Pittsburgh.

As a male citizen of Korea, I'm currently back home serving my mandatory military conscription. Fortunately, I was able to put my education to use as an alternative civil service in lieu of physically training to be a foot soldier. I am conducting research at a small-sized environmental company Siontech Co., Ltd. My work focuses on water purification and plastic recycle technolgies. Thankfully, working as a researcher for my service, I get time after work to myself with some restrictions. I like to spend those times working on different projects and learning new things. I also enjoy playing hockey and other sports, as well as playing video games.

During the time I was abroad, I was fortunate to encounter many brilliant people, diverse cultures, and fascinating bodies of thought. I'm hoping that once I fulfill my service to my country, I get to countinue learning about the world and meet more great minds. That includes traveling around the world, live in differnt places, and interacting with people from all types of background.
