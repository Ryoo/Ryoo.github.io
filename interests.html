Introducing Myself

I was born in Daejeon, South Korea. For those who are not from South Korea and not familiar with Dajeon, Dajeon is a city surrounded by mountains and it has many Korea's national science facilities. Also having national science museum and other science/technology related places, Daejeon was a great place to grow my passion and interest in science. At the end of my middle school, I wanted to study abroad in United States. I wanted to be able to speak English fluently, so that I can conduct research with the world-class researchers while learning about the other side of the world. It was not easy to convince my parents, but they had faith in me. With my parents full support, I was able to live in many places and educations in U.S. including Utah, Connecticut and Pennylvania, where I was able to finish my Masters degree under guidence of Dr. Millstone in University of Pittsburgh.

As a Korean man, I'm currently back home serving my military conscription. Fortunately, I was able to put in use of my education as an alternative civil sevice in lieu of physically training to be a foot soldier. I am researching in a small-sized environmental company Siontech Co., Ltd. My work focuses on water purification technology, which I hope can be used to help those in need of clean water and also keep the envrionment healthy. Working as a researcher for my service, I get after work-time to myself with some restrictions. I like to spend those times working on different projects and learning new things. I also enjoy playing hockey and other sports, as well as playing video games.
